The currency of the future

Here's a timeline of bitcoin:

  • 2008 - The domain bitcoin.org is registered and Satoshi Nakamoto publishes the programming fundamentals of the virtual payment system.
  • 2009 - Bitcoin's P2P network comes into operation with the creation of the first open source computer application and the production of the genesis block, the first entry in the digital currency's transaction record.
  • 2010 - A Florida programmer makes the first purchase of a product with "bitcoins" when buying a pizza.
  • 2011 - The bitcoin reaches parity with the dollar and then plummets to a cent of the dollar after the theft of 600 accounts of Mt. Gox.
  • 2012 - The European Central Bank (ECB) links a possible expansion of virtual currency with a threat to the traditional financial system.
  • 2013 - The price of bitcoin exceeds $ 1,000 and reaches parity with the price of an ounce of gold.
  • 2014 - The first ATM for the purchase and sale of bitcoins is installed in Spain.
  • 2015 - The Australian police search the home of one of the alleged creators of bitcoin, Craig Wright.
  • 2016 - Australian businessman Craig Wright identifies himself as Satoshi Nakamoto, the creator of bitcoin and the total value of bitcoins in circulation exceeds $ 14 billion, according to Reuters.
  • 2017 - The most important event in history when after a period of sustained rise in value, the digital currency surpassed the barrier of USD $ 19,000.
  • 2018 - Bitcoin falls to $ 6,330 after costing $ 20,000 in December 2017, then falls below $ 3,000 and rises again to close to $ 6,000.
  • 2019 - Support for altruistic watchtowers is added to the Lightning LND daemon. Altruistic watchtowers do not receive rewards from the protocol to protect customer channels, so users themselves must run their own watchtower or rely on the generosity of a watchtower operator.
  • 2020 - Bitcoin breaks its all-time high and is currently trading above $ 28,000, with a capitalization of more than 540 billion dollars.
